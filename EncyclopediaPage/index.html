Saltwater Crocidile

The Saltwater Crocidile is the largest known reptile speices in the world. Males can grow up to 20 feet in length and weigh 2,200 to 2,900 lbs. They inhabit saltwater enviorments and wetlands in India, Southeast Asia and Austrlia. It is classified as a large hypercarnivious oppurtnunistic apex predator. They have an extensive diet of crustaceans, other reptiles, birds, fish, and mammals.

Behavior and Habitat

In warmer seasons the speices is more active and aggressive, while remaing lethargic in the colder months to help it survuve long periods of time without food. They typically bask in the day time and hunt at night. Most attacks on humans can be linked to warmer seasons and also more frequnetly occuring duirng the night. They mainly dwell in costal waterways like rivers, streams, canals or wetlands and lakes close to coasts. These water ways that contain currents play a vital role in their conservation of enrgy allowing them to ride curretns to new destinations.
